A cookie is a small text file your browser stores when you visit a website. It can hold a session ID, a preference, or a tracking identifier. Cookies make websites remember things between visits — like staying logged in or knowing which dates you searched.

@if (isOpen(2)) {

Strictly necessary

These keep the site working — for example, keeping you signed in, remembering items in your cart, and protecting against cross-site request forgery. You can't opt out; the site won't function without them.

Functional

These remember your preferences (such as locally saved favorites or display settings). Disabling them won't break the site but may reset choices on each visit.

Analytics

We may use privacy-respecting analytics to understand aggregate trends — how many visitors, which pages are most useful, and where they came from. Where used, our analytics do not track individual users across sites.

Marketing & advertising

We don't currently use third-party advertising or remarketing cookies. If that changes we'll update this policy.
